Content & Axes

A Window to Knowledge & Identity

Every topic is addressed through four integrated axes, making the text not just reading material, but a window to knowledge and identity.

Local

Connects the learner to their national and Arabic identity with a progression that respects each nation's uniqueness. It covers: civilization, traditions, landmarks, inspiring personalities, local history, geography, arts, and literature.

Historical

Windows into the great civilizations that shaped humanity, both ancient and modern. It covers: Arab-Islamic, Pharaonic, Roman, Greek, Chinese, Indian, and the Modern Era.

Subject

Structured knowledge across two major streams covering the realms of science and humanity. It covers: Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM), and Humanities, Social Sciences, Arts, and Literature (HASS).

Islamic

Connects the learner to their Islamic reference through its authentic texts, authored under strict pedagogical guidelines. It covers: the Holy Quran, Prophetic Hadith, Islamic Jurisprudence (Fiqh), and the Prophet's Biography (Seerah).

Personalities

Every Topic is Paired with an Inspiring Role Model

Intersecting the four axes is a layer of classified personalities (scientific, literary, political, religious, social, artistic, athletic...). By pairing conceptual topics with relatable human stories, learning becomes an engaging narrative rather than rote instruction.
